Quick Shift is a mode seeking algorithm (like mean shift) which instead of iteratively shifting each point towards a local mean instead forms a tree of links to the nearest neighbor which increases the density.

This demo shows Quick Shift in a simple superpixelization problem where we seek to segment this image:

The image we wish to segment

As a feature vector, we choose the LAB colorspace representation of the image augmented with the x,y location of the pixel. vl_quickseg is a convenient wrapper function which takes care of the transformation of the image to LAB and performs a segmentation, making our job as easy as:

Iseg = vl_quickseg(I, ratio, sigma, tau);
Two segmentations of the original image with differing vaues for tau. On the left, tau = 10, the right, tau = 30.
